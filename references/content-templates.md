```
Yes. [Financing terms] on approved credit for [minimum amount]. We accept all major credit cards, checks, and cash. [Payment options shouldn't stop you from getting the service you need].

```
[Service] involves [complexity factors]. The price includes [what's included]. We use [quality level] materials and [warranty terms]. Cheaper options often lead to [problems] within [timeframe].

## Explanation Text by Block

### SAFETY_NOTICE

**Pattern**: [Danger description] + [How to recognize] + [Why it matters]

```
[Gas/CO/Hazard] is [colorless/odorless/invisible]. If you notice [symptoms], your [system] may have [problem]: a safety hazard requiring immediate attention. [Action to take now].
```

**Examples**:
- CO: "Carbon monoxide is colorless and odorless. If you notice soot buildup, burning smells, or your family feels lightheaded, your system may have incomplete combustion: a safety hazard requiring immediate attention."
- Gas leak: "Natural gas has a rotten egg smell. If you smell gas, hear hissing, or see dead plants near gas lines, evacuate immediately and call 911 then us."

### SYMPTOM_MATRIX

**Pattern**: [Empathize with confusion] + [Value of self-diagnosis] + [What they can do with info]

```
Is your [system] acting up? Here's what those [symptoms] usually mean, and what it typically costs to fix. Use this to decide if you need emergency service or can wait until [timeframe].
```

### PATH_CHOICE

**Pattern**: [Acknowledge decision difficulty] + [Show you'll help] + [Remove pressure]

```
[Decision question]? We'll walk you through the math with no pressure either way. Our job is to give you the information to make the right choice for your [situation].
```

**Examples**:
- "Should you repair or replace your 12-year-old heater? We'll walk you through the math with no pressure either way."
- "Wondering if you need emergency service or can wait? Here's how to decide based on severity and safety."

### PRICING_TABLE

**Pattern**: [Acknowledge price anxiety] + [Your transparency approach] + [Risk reversal]

```
No surprises. Every [service] starts with [diagnostic process]. We show you the problem, explain your options, and only proceed with your approval. If we can't fix it, you pay nothing.
```

### TESTIMONIAL

**Pattern**: [Specific situation] + [Your response] + [Outcome] + [Emotional result]

```
[Problem happened at worst time]. [Company] came [timeframe] and [action taken]. [Tech name] explained everything and [didn't do pressure tactic]. [Final result].
```

**Examples**:
- "Furnace died during the February freeze. They came within 2 hours and had us warm again by dinner. The tech explained everything and didn't upsell us."
- "Pipe burst at 11pm on a Sunday. Mike answered immediately, arrived in 30 minutes, and stopped the leak. Fair price, no emergency gouging."

## Copy Voice by Niche

### HVAC/Plumbing/Roofing (Home Services)

```yaml
tone: Direct, confident, action-oriented
sentence_length: Short to medium
technical_level: Explain simply but don't condescend
urgency: Appropriate to situation (high for emergency, moderate for standard)

patterns:
  - Active voice: "We fix it" not "It gets fixed"
  - Concrete specifics: "within 2 hours" not "quickly"
  - Problem → solution: "Heater down? We're on it."
```

**Good**:
- "No heat? We're on it. Call now for same-day emergency repair."
- "Licensed technicians carry common parts for same-visit repairs."

**Bad**:
- "Experience the ultimate in heating comfort solutions."
- "Your HVAC system requires periodic maintenance."

### Dental/Medical

```yaml
tone: Gentle, reassuring, empathetic
sentence_length: Medium
technical_level: Explain clearly, address anxiety
urgency: Low to medium (avoid pressure)

patterns:
  - Comfort focus: "gentle", "comfortable", "pain-free"
  - Partnership: "together we'll", "we'll help you"
  - Transparency: "here's what to expect", "we'll explain"
```

**Good**:
- "Gentle dental care designed around your comfort and schedule."
- "We'll explain every step and answer all your questions before we begin."

**Bad**:
- "Act now! Limited slots for this procedure!"
- "You need this treatment immediately or else..."

**AHPRA compliance (Australian medical/dental only):**
These restrictions apply ONLY when the business is both (1) medical or dental AND (2) located in Australia. For non-Australian or non-medical businesses, ignore this entirely. Under AHPRA, "pain-free", "painless", and "gentle" are restricted minimisers. Use measurable benefits instead: "less bleeding", "smoother recovery", "no tooth preparation required".

### Pediatric/Family Medical

```yaml
tone: Warm, reassuring, parent-empathetic
sentence_length: Medium
technical_level: Explain clearly, address parental anxiety
urgency: Medium (sick visits are urgent but not emergencies)

patterns:
  - Parent framing: "your child" not "the patient"
  - Reassurance: "we see this often", "most children recover quickly"
  - Speed: "same-day sick visits", "call before 4pm"
  - Credentials: board certifications, languages spoken
```

**Good**:
- "Same-day sick visits for your child with board-certified pediatricians."
- "We see ear infections every day. Here is what to watch for and when to call us."

**Bad**:
- "Bring in your patient for evaluation of otitis media."
- "Act now! Your child's health depends on it!"

### Legal (Personal Injury)

```yaml
tone: Assertive but supportive, empowering
sentence_length: Medium to long (explain complexities)
technical_level: Accessible but professional
urgency: Medium (statute of limitations creates real urgency)

patterns:
  - Empowerment: "your rights", "you deserve", "we fight for you"
  - Transparency: "here's how it works", "no fees unless we win"
  - Outcome-focused: "maximum compensation", "get what you deserve"
```

**Good**:
- "Fighting for accident victims in Dallas since 1998. No fees unless we win your case."
- "Insurance companies have lawyers. You should too."

**Bad**:
- "Guaranteed million-dollar settlement!" (can't guarantee)
- "Sue now and get rich!" (inappropriate tone)

### Financial Services

```yaml
tone: Professional, conservative, educational
sentence_length: Medium to long (explain complexities)
technical_level: Clear but sophisticated
urgency: Very low (avoid pressure tactics)

patterns:
  - Education: "here's how", "consider", "understand"
  - Transparency: All fees visible, risks disclosed
  - Long-term: "plan", "strategy", "future"
```

**Good**:
- "Understanding your mortgage options: rates, terms, and closing costs explained."
- "APR: 4.25% (4.38% with fees). All costs itemized below."

**Bad**:
- "Limited time offer! Rates won't last!"
- "Get rich quick with our investment strategy!"

## Word Choice Guidelines

### Power Words by Intent

**Emergency/Urgent**:
- Fast, immediate, now, emergency, rapid, quick
- Stop (damage), protect, safe
- Available, ready, on-call

**Trust-building**:
- Licensed, certified, insured, experienced
- Honest, transparent, upfront
- Guaranteed, warrantied, proven

**Decision Support**:
- Explain, understand, compare, decide
- Options, choices, help
- No pressure, your decision

### Words to Avoid

**All niches**:
- "Cheap" (say "affordable" or "competitive")
- "Best" without proof (say "highly rated" with number)
- Superlatives without basis ("world's greatest")

**All niches (AI writing tells)**:
- Em-dashes (—) and en-dashes (–) : use colons, commas, periods, or parentheses. No U+2014 or U+2013 characters anywhere in output.
- "Nestled in" : cliche geo writing
- "Look no further" : cliche CTA
- "We pride ourselves on..." : self-congratulatory filler
- "State-of-the-art" : meaningless without specifics
- "Your premier..." : generic filler
- "Comprehensive solutions" : says nothing

**Emergency services**:
- "Maybe", "might", "possibly" (undermines confidence)
- "Try" (say "fix" or "repair")

**High-consideration services**:
- Pressure words ("act now", "limited time") without legitimate reason
- Absolute guarantees you can't keep

## Formatting Guidelines

### Sentence Length

**Emergency pages**: Short sentences (10-15 words average)
```
Your heater is down. We get it. Call now. We'll be there fast.
```

**Research pages**: Medium sentences (15-25 words average)
```
When deciding between repair and replacement, consider the age of your system, recent repair history, and current efficiency ratings.
```

### Paragraph Length

- **Mobile**: 2-3 sentences max per paragraph
- **Desktop**: 3-5 sentences max per paragraph
- **Always**: Break up long blocks with subheads, bullets, or whitespace

### List Formatting

**Use bullet lists for**:
- Features or benefits (parallel structure)
- Steps in a process (numbered)
- Options or choices

**Use inline text for**:
- Narrative flow
- Explanations requiring context
- Emotional storytelling

## A/B Test Variations

### Headlines

**Test**: Urgency vs Trust
- A: "Emergency Heater Repair McKinney TX" (urgency)
- B: "Licensed Heater Repair McKinney Since 1987" (trust)

**Test**: Benefit vs Feature
- A: "Back to Warmth Within Hours" (benefit)
- B: "Same-Day Heater Repair Service" (feature)

### CTAs

**Test**: Direct vs Benefit
- A: "Call Now" (direct)
- B: "Get Help Fast" (benefit)

**Test**: Formal vs Conversational
- A: "Request Service" (formal)
- B: "Get Started" (conversational)

### Explanation Text

**Test**: Short vs Detailed
- A: 2 sentences, focus on outcome
- B: 4 sentences, include process

**Test**: You-focused vs We-focused
- A: "Your heater fixed fast" (you)
- B: "We fix your heater fast" (we)

## Anti-Slop Content Rules

Five codified rules to prevent generic, low-quality content. Apply to every block during content population.

### Rule 1: Block Suppression Over Generic Content

If a block's required data cannot be populated with real, specific, verifiable information for this business, suppress the block entirely. A missing block is always less damaging than a generic one.

```
GOOD: Page with 12 strong blocks
BAD: Page with 15 blocks where 3 are filled with filler
```

### Rule 2: Ban Vague Adjectives Without Evidence

These words are FORBIDDEN as standalone claims: "best", "quality", "professional", "experienced", "trusted", "expert", "state-of-the-art", "premier", "comprehensive". Each requires a verifiable backing:

```
BAD: "Our experienced team provides quality service"
GOOD: "Our 5 lead technicians average 12 years in the trade, with a 2-year warranty on all work"

BAD: "Trusted by the community"
GOOD: "4.9 stars from 200+ Google reviews in McKinney"
```

### Rule 3: Landmark and Local Reference Requirement

Every page needs 2+ specific local references that prove you actually know the area:

- Named neighborhoods, streets, or landmarks
- Local code numbers or regulatory bodies
- Environmental factors specific to the area (soil type, climate, pollen season)
- Named local institutions (hospitals, schools, major employers)

```
BAD: "Serving the local community"
GOOD: "Located near Baylor Medical Center at Warren Pkwy, serving 75033, 75034, 75035"
```

### Rule 4: Quantify or Kill

Every claim needs a number, policy, named entity, or timeline. If it can't be quantified, remove it.

```
BAD: "We respond fast" → KILL IT
GOOD: "We respond within 90 minutes in the DFW area"

BAD: "Affordable pricing" → KILL IT
GOOD: "$89 diagnostic fee, waived with repair"

BAD: "Years of experience" → KILL IT
GOOD: "Serving North Texas since 1987 (38 years)"
```

### Rule 5: Falsifiability Test

Could a competitor copy this sentence verbatim onto their own page without changing a word? If yes, rewrite or remove it.

```
FAILS: "We provide honest, reliable service" (any business could say this)
PASSES: "We only book 6 jobs per tech per day so we can spend 90 minutes per visit"

FAILS: "Your satisfaction is our priority"
PASSES: "If we can't fix it on the first visit, the diagnostic fee is refunded"
```

## Progressive CTA Copy Patterns

CTA text must evolve as the reader scrolls and accumulates trust. Each position targets a different psychological state. Never use the same CTA text twice on a page.

**Phone number CTA rule:** The bare phone number (e.g., "Call (512) 555-0147") should appear as button text at most once (typically in the hero). Every subsequent phone CTA must wrap the number in different context text:
- Hero: "Call (512) 555-0147"
- Mid-page: "Speak to Our Team"
- After reviews: "Talk to a Licensed Plumber"
- Final CTA: "Ready? Call Us Now"
The `href="tel:..."` link can repeat, but the visible button text must not.

### Position 1: Hero (Zero Trust)

Match dominant intent. No social proof yet, so be direct.

```yaml
emergency: "Call (XXX) XXX-XXXX"
soon: "Schedule Service Today"
planning: "Get Free Consultation"
research: "Learn About [Service] Options"
```

### Position 2: After Service/Condition Content (Knows What You Do)

Narrow to their likely specific need based on what they just read.

```yaml
examples:
  - "Schedule Your Ear Infection Visit"
  - "Get a Quote for Furnace Repair"
  - "Book Your Implant Consultation"
```

### Position 3: Mid-Page CTA Bar (Has Differentiation + Some Trust)

Leverage accumulated social proof. Slim bar, not full section.

```yaml
examples:
  - "Join 500+ [City] families who trust us"
  - "Trusted by [City] patients since [year]"
  - "4.9 stars from 200+ Google reviews"
```

### Position 4: After Reviews (Seen Peer Validation)

Address the remaining objection that's keeping them from converting.

```yaml
examples:
  - "No obligation, no pressure"
  - "Free consultation, honest advice"
  - "Same-day appointments available"
```

### Position 5: Final CTA (Consumed Everything)

Reference specific value prop from the page. Never use generic "Contact Us".

```yaml
examples:
  - "Ready for same-day relief in [City]?"
  - "Get your [specific benefit] today"
  - "Your [service] is one call away"

anti_patterns:
  - "Contact Us Today" (generic)
  - "Get Started" (says nothing)
  - "Learn More" (they already learned)
