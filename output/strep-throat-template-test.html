Is It Strep Throat or a Viral Sore Throat?

Not every sore throat is strep. Here is how to tell the difference so you know whether your child needs a same-day visit or can recover at home.

Symptom Strep Throat (Bacterial) Viral Sore Throat
Onset Sudden, severe sore throat Gradual, mild to moderate
Fever 101F or higher, often 103F+ Low-grade or no fever
Tonsils Red, swollen with white patches or pus May be red, no white patches
Cough Absent (key differentiator) Often present with runny nose
Lymph Nodes Tender, swollen neck nodes Mildly swollen or normal
Other Signs Headache, stomachache, red spots on palate (petechiae), possible rash Sneezing, congestion, watery eyes
Age Group Most common ages 5 to 15 Any age
Treatment Requires antibiotics (typically amoxicillin, 10-day course) Rest, fluids, symptom management (no antibiotics)
Action Needed Same-day visit for rapid strep test Monitor at home, visit if symptoms worsen after 3 to 5 days

Signs It May Be Strep

  • Sudden, severe sore throat without cough
  • Fever 101F or higher
  • White patches on tonsils
  • Swollen, tender neck lymph nodes
  • Headache or stomachache
  • Red spots on roof of mouth

Signs It Is Likely Viral

  • Gradual onset with cough and runny nose
  • Low-grade or no fever
  • Sneezing, congestion, watery eyes
  • No white patches on tonsils
Important: The only way to confirm strep throat is with a test. Many children with strep have no cough, which is a reliable clue, but a rapid strep test takes just 10 minutes and removes the guesswork.

What Should You Do Right Now?

Use this guide to decide whether your child needs immediate care or can wait. When in doubt, call us at (469) 425-3600 and we will help you decide.

Go to the ER

  • Severe difficulty breathing or swallowing
  • Drooling and unable to swallow saliva
  • Stiff neck with high fever
  • Signs of dehydration (no urination for 8+ hours)

Same-Day Visit

  • Sore throat with fever over 101F
  • White patches visible on tonsils
  • Known exposure to strep at school or daycare
  • Sore throat lasting more than 48 hours
  • Rash with sore throat (scarlet fever screening)

Watch at Home

  • Mild sore throat with cough and runny nose
  • No fever or low-grade fever under 101F
  • Child is drinking fluids and eating
  • Symptoms improving after 1 to 2 days

If home symptoms worsen or fever develops, call us to schedule a same-day visit.

Why Strep Throat Peaks in Frisco from November Through March

Frisco's school density and seasonal patterns create conditions for strep outbreaks every winter. Here is what local parents should know.

Peak Season: November to March

Strep throat cases in North Texas spike between November and March, coinciding with the school year and cooler indoor air. Group A Streptococcus bacteria spread most easily in close-contact environments during this window. We see the highest volume of positive strep tests in January and February at our Frisco office.

School & Daycare Transmission

Frisco ISD is one of the fastest-growing districts in Texas, and classroom density increases exposure risk. We regularly treat clusters of strep cases from elementary schools and daycares in Phillips Creek Ranch, Richwoods, and Lexington Country. When one case is confirmed, siblings and close contacts should be watched for symptoms.

Reinfection and Carriers

Some children get strep multiple times per season. About 20% of school-age children are asymptomatic carriers, meaning they carry the bacteria in their throat without symptoms but can spread it to classmates. If your child keeps getting strep, we can discuss carrier testing and management strategies.

Untreated Strep Risks

Left untreated, strep can lead to peritonsillar abscess, rheumatic fever (which can damage heart valves), and post-streptococcal glomerulonephritis (kidney inflammation). These complications are rare when antibiotics are started within 9 days of symptom onset, which is why prompt testing matters.

Frequently Asked Questions About Strep Throat in Children

How quickly will I get strep test results?

Our in-office rapid strep test produces results in 10 minutes. If the rapid test is negative but your child's symptoms strongly suggest strep, we send a backup throat culture to the lab. Culture results typically come back within 24 to 48 hours, and we call you as soon as they are available.

What antibiotics are prescribed for strep throat?

The standard treatment is amoxicillin taken for 10 days. For children with penicillin allergies, we prescribe alternatives such as azithromycin or cephalexin. It is critical to complete the full course of antibiotics even after your child feels better, which usually happens within 2 to 3 days. Stopping early increases the risk of the infection returning and of complications like rheumatic fever.

When can my child go back to school after strep?

Per Frisco ISD's illness policy, your child may return to school after being on antibiotics for at least 24 hours and being fever-free for 24 hours without the use of fever-reducing medications (ibuprofen, acetaminophen). Most children with strep can return to school on the second full day of treatment. We can provide a doctor's note for the school if needed.

Can strep throat be dangerous if left untreated?

Yes. Untreated strep can lead to peritonsillar abscess (a pocket of pus behind the tonsil), rheumatic fever (which can permanently damage heart valves), and post-streptococcal glomerulonephritis (kidney inflammation). These complications are preventable with timely antibiotic treatment. This is why we recommend testing whenever strep is suspected, rather than waiting to see if symptoms resolve on their own.

Do you accept my insurance?

Entirely Kids Pediatrics accepts most major insurance plans. Call our office at (469) 425-3600 with your insurance details and we will verify your coverage before your visit. Sick visits, including the rapid strep test, are typically covered under your plan's office visit copay.

My child keeps getting strep. What can we do?

Recurrent strep (defined as 7 or more episodes in one year, or 5+ in each of the past 2 years, or 3+ in each of the past 3 years) may warrant a referral to an ENT for tonsillectomy evaluation. We also assess whether your child may be a strep carrier or is being re-exposed at school or daycare. Replacing toothbrushes after a strep diagnosis and treating any household contacts with symptoms can also reduce reinfection.

Can I get a telemedicine visit for strep throat?

Strep throat requires a throat swab for diagnosis, so the initial visit must be in-person. However, we offer telemedicine follow-ups if your child is not improving after 48 to 72 hours on antibiotics, or for questions about symptoms during recovery.
