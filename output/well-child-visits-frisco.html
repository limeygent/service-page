What Happens at a Well-Child Visit

A typical well-child visit lasts 20-30 minutes. Here is what to expect at each appointment.

1

Growth Measurements

We measure height, weight, and head circumference (for infants), then plot them on age-appropriate growth charts. For children age 2 and older, we calculate BMI percentile to identify any nutritional concerns early.

2

Developmental Milestone Review

Your doctor reviews your child's speech, motor skills, social behavior, and cognitive development using validated screening tools. This includes the M-CHAT autism screening at 18 and 24 months, and the PHQ-A depression screen for adolescents.

3

Physical Examination

A thorough head-to-toe exam checks eyes, ears, heart, lungs, abdomen, skin, and musculoskeletal system. Vision screening begins at age 3, hearing screening at age 4, and scoliosis checks start at age 10.

4

Vaccinations

We administer age-appropriate immunizations per the CDC schedule. We explain each vaccine, discuss any concerns you have, and provide the Vaccine Information Statement (VIS) for every shot. Texas DSHS requires specific vaccines for school and daycare enrollment.

5

Anticipatory Guidance

Your doctor discusses age-specific topics: safe sleep for infants, nutrition and picky eating for toddlers, screen time guidelines, puberty preparation, and mental health for teens. This is also the time to ask any questions on your mind.

6

School & Sports Forms

We complete all required forms during the visit: Texas DSHS immunization records, Frisco ISD enrollment health forms, UIL sports physical clearance (valid for 1 calendar year), and daycare/camp medical forms. No separate appointment needed.

Developmental Milestones We Track

Every well-child visit includes a developmental assessment. Here are the key milestones we evaluate at each stage, so you know what your doctor is looking for and what questions to ask.

0-12 Months

  • Lifts head, rolls over, sits up
  • Reaches for objects, transfers hand to hand
  • Babbles, responds to name
  • Social smile, stranger awareness
  • First foods introduction (4-6 months)

1-3 Years

  • Walking, running, climbing stairs
  • First words, 2-word phrases by age 2
  • Follows simple directions
  • Parallel play, emerging independence
  • Toilet training readiness signs

4-10 Years

  • Writes name, reads age-appropriate text
  • Cooperative play, friendships
  • Follows multi-step instructions
  • Attention span for school tasks
  • Physical coordination for sports

11-18 Years

  • Puberty stages (Tanner staging)
  • Academic and social adjustment
  • Mental health screening (PHQ-A)
  • Substance use awareness
  • Confidential adolescent time with doctor

Pediatric Care Designed for Frisco Families

Frisco is one of the fastest-growing cities in North Texas, with a population over 230,000 and a median age under 35. That means a lot of young families navigating well-child visits, school enrollment requirements, and sports physicals for the first time.

Frisco ISD Requirements

Frisco ISD requires up-to-date immunization records for enrollment and an annual health screening. We complete all FISD enrollment forms during your child's well-child visit, including the Texas Certificate of Immunization. For students entering 7th grade, the Tdap and meningococcal vaccines are required by Texas law before the first day of school.

Sports Physicals for FISD Athletics

UIL requires a physical exam within the past calendar year for all student athletes. For FISD middle and high school sports (Wakeland, Liberty, Centennial, Lebanon Trail, Heritage, Lone Star, Reedy, Panther Creek, Emerson, and Memorial), we complete the UIL Pre-Participation Physical Evaluation form during any well-child visit or as a standalone appointment. Schedule early: physicals fill up fast in July and August.

North Texas Seasonal Health

Frisco's DFW climate means specific seasonal health patterns: cedar/mountain juniper pollen (December through February), ragweed (August through November), grass pollen (April through June), RSV season peaking in fall, and flu season November through March. We adjust our screening and guidance to match what Frisco kids are actually experiencing each season.

Texas DSHS Immunization Requirements

The Texas Department of State Health Services (DSHS) mandates specific immunizations for school and daycare attendance. Required vaccines include:

  • DTaP/Tdap (5 doses)
  • Polio/IPV (4 doses)
  • MMR (2 doses)
  • Hepatitis B (3 doses)
  • Varicella (2 doses)
  • Hepatitis A (2 doses)
  • Meningococcal (ages 11-12)
  • Tdap booster (7th grade)

We track your child's immunization record against DSHS requirements and flag any gaps before they become a school enrollment issue.

Frequently Asked Questions About Well-Child Visits

How often does my child need a well-child visit?
The AAP recommends 7 visits in the first year (at 3-5 days, 1 month, 2 months, 4 months, 6 months, 9 months, and 12 months), then visits at 15, 18, 24, and 30 months, age 3, and then annually from age 4 through 18. That totals about 25-27 well-child visits from birth through high school graduation.
Will my insurance cover the well-child visit?
Most insurance plans cover well-child visits at 100% as preventive care under the ACA. This includes the exam, immunizations, and standard screenings. If a medical issue is diagnosed and treated during the visit (beyond the scope of the preventive exam), that portion may have a copay. We verify your benefits before your appointment and explain any potential costs upfront.
Can I get a sports physical at the same time as a well-child visit?
Yes. We complete the UIL Pre-Participation Physical Evaluation form during any well-child visit at no additional charge. The physical is valid for one calendar year. For FISD athletes, we recommend scheduling the well-child visit in the spring or early summer so the physical is valid for the entire upcoming school sports season.
What if I have questions about vaccines?
We welcome vaccine questions. Dr. Leung and Dr. Cheng take time to explain each recommended vaccine, its purpose, and the evidence behind it. We follow the CDC and AAP immunization schedule and can discuss the reasoning behind any specific vaccine. Texas DSHS requires certain immunizations for school enrollment, and we help you stay in compliance so your child is never held out of class for missing records.
Will my child see the same doctor every time?
Yes. We follow a same-doctor continuity model. When you choose Dr. Leung or Dr. Cheng as your child's pediatrician, your child sees that same physician at every well-child visit and sick visit. This means your doctor knows your child's full medical history, temperament, and developmental trajectory without starting from scratch each time.
What school forms can you complete during the visit?
We complete all standard forms during the well-child visit: Texas Certificate of Immunization (required by DSHS for school and daycare enrollment), FISD Student Health History form, UIL sports physical form, daycare medical forms, and camp health forms. Bring any blank forms with you and we will fill them out that day.
Do you offer telemedicine for follow-ups?
Yes. After an in-person well-child visit, we offer telemedicine follow-ups for non-urgent concerns like reviewing lab results, discussing developmental questions, or checking in on a previously identified issue. The initial well-child exam must be done in-person for accurate growth measurements and physical examination.
When should I schedule my child's well-child visit?
For infants, visits are scheduled around the AAP-recommended ages (the exact timing matters for vaccine schedules). For school-age children and teens, we recommend scheduling the annual well-child visit in April through June, before the back-to-school rush. This gives you time to catch up on any needed immunizations and complete sports physicals before FISD deadlines. July and August appointments fill quickly.
What languages do your doctors speak?
Dr. Danielle Leung speaks English and Cantonese. Dr. Karen Cheng speaks English and Mandarin. Frisco has a significant multilingual population, and we want parents to feel comfortable discussing their child's health in their preferred language whenever possible.
Are you accepting new patients?
Yes. We are currently accepting new patients of all ages, from newborns to 18-year-olds. Call (469) 425-3600 to schedule your child's first visit. We typically schedule new patient appointments within 1-2 weeks.
